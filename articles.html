WHAT EVEN HAPPENED AT THE OSCARS?

An article by Lova Venema

email: lova.venema@gmail.com

Every year, as the new year begins to take shape, there is always one thing you can count on, and that is, awards season. With this last year’s film boom, there were quite a few works that were worthy competitors for the various awards. The run of red carpets came to its close this last Saturday as the Oscar’s took place at the Dolby Theatre in Hollywood, LA. What is normally a very classy and well directed affair with a star-studded red carpet, a precise manuscript and set menu, was something else altogether this year. What even happened at the Oscars?

The night begun as any oscars eve. Stars arrived to the carpet an lined up to have their pictures taken. Reporters were ready with their tiny microphones and cameras, the crowd on the other side of the street was flocking to catch a glimpse of the celbrities. The troubles started when the cast of the latest Marvel film adroned the red steps. The film has gained a lot of traction due to the drama between the lead actress, Millie Lou, and the two male leads Steven Johnsson and Mike King. Lou and King are rumored to have had an affair during filming, which supposedly made Johnsson very mad, causing a falling out between Johnsson and King. When the cast adorned the carpet, an argument broke out between King and Johnsson that resulted in both of them being removed by security, the two actors were not seen during the dinner and were not present during the awards ceremony.

Moving on from the disaster carpet, stars entered the Dolby theatre and started to find their seats. Everyone sat down, began their dinner and the cermenoy started. This year’s host, Jean Villerunie, who’s harsh opionions on hollywood and actors does not go quietly by had the audience somewhat uncomfortable in their seats. He opened his monologue by making a few jokes based on the celebreties, which had many people laughing.

But then, Villerunie makes a joke about the recent split between hollywoods favorite couple Margret Kane and Brent Sacker. The joke made it out to sound like that it was Kane’s fault that Sacker left, when it was Sacker who had multiple affairs during the marrige. This joke caused a very strong reaction with Sacker, who left his seat, went up on stage and punched Villerunie in the face. The cermony took a brief intermission and Sacker too had to leave the Dolby theatre.

One might think this is where the disasters stopped here, but no. Villerunie’s face was in such bad shape that he had to leave the Dolby theatre to seek medical attention. This left the Oscar’s without a host to keep guests entertained between awards. Thus the rest of the gala was just akward if you ask this reporter. Many of the performances fell flat without the comedic tone the that the gala often prompts for. Everyone recieved their awards and the ceremony fisnished about 40 minutes earlier that planned. The nightmare evening that was this year’s oscars did however make many of the stars let loose during the afterparty, which according to sources was the best one since Elton John lit a piano on fire in 1996. In conclusion, this year’s oscars was a real lowpoint in the academys almost 100 year history, granted however it was not at the fault of the academy, but rather the drama of grown up men and women. But it did prove that a real lowpoint sparks a great afterparty.
