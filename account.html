Bio

I’ve always been the last person to leave the theater. The lights come on, everyone else gets up, and I’m still there, thinking about the characters and the world they left behind. I don’t write reviews because I need to impress anyone—I write because I need to understand what I’ve just watched. For me, movies aren’t about spectacle; they’re about the way they make you feel long after the credits roll. If you’re the kind of person who likes to sit in silence after a film, maybe we’ll get along.

TOP REVIEWS

WE LIVE IN TIME

Rating: 8.25

This one quietly devastates. Garfield and Pugh move through a story told in fragments—love, illness, memory—all folded together like pages of a letter you’ve read too many times. It’s not about what happens, but how time makes us remember it. No melodrama, just the ache of real life unfolding. A film for when you’re feeling soft and a little lost.

CHALLENGERS

Rating: 7.86

Not a sports movie. Not really a romance either. This is a pressure cooker disguised as a love triangle. Zendaya controls the court—both literally and emotionally—while the two men spiral around her like they’re addicted to losing. Guadagnino shoots desire like it’s a weapon. You don’t watch Challengers, you tense through it.

THE SHINING

Rating: 9.37

Every time I return to this film, I feel the hotel watching me. It’s not just horror—it’s the sound of isolation, the dread of silence, the slow crack of sanity. Nicholson doesn’t descend into madness; he’s already part of the Overlook’s design. Kubrick lets nothing feel safe, not even time. It’s cold, precise, and endlessly rewatchable. A masterpiece of stillness and suggestion.
